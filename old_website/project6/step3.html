Step 3

Change the jump probability \(q\) to (a) 0 and (b) 0.5. Describe the resulting changes in the page rank. What is the purpose of the jump probability?

Note that column 1 and column 3 correspond to the cases \(p=0\) and \(p=0.5\), respectively. Here we can see in the rows whose entries have high (respectively, low) probability (see for example, rows 13, 14, and 15 (respectively, rows 1,2,3)), the probabilities decrease (respectively, increase). These both converge to \(\frac{1}{15}\), where 15 is the number of rows. The purpose of the jump probability is how likely someone is to click a link on a page. So the closer the probability is to 1, the more evenly distributed the probability that one will click on a link will be.
