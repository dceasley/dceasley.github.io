  7. Using the same parameters as in part 2, with the power being \(5\, W\), the boundary conditions were set everywhere to the neutral conditions, i.e. no power being supplied. However, a \(2\times 2\) square in the center had power added to it. The equations themselves were not modified as the heat conduction and convection remained the same, but the solution to the equations was set to the power condition as opposed to zero.

    Interestingly enough, it seems that the heat distribution is perfectly linear from the true center of the chip within a disk encompassed entirely on the cooling fin. This models the chip being placed at the center of the cooling fin. However, the maximum heat, and in fact, the average heat, seems significantly greater than when chip is connected to the side, likely due to more points being affected by the placement here. This model assumes that the chip has the same properties as the cooling fin.
